Section 5.3 of Part 5 specifically deals with the 'Definitions' and it refers to the term "Hazard" as a condition that could foreseeably cause, or contribute to, an aircraft accident.

The FAA (Federal Aviation Administration) emphasizes that hazards are conditions, events, or circumstances that could lead to or contribute to an unplanned or undesired event. 

It's important to note that a hazard in itself doesn't cause harm but is a condition that, if left uncontrolled, can lead to an accident or incident. For example, a bird near an airport isn't a hazard, but it becomes one if it's on a collision course with an aircraft. 

The identification and management of hazards is a critical component of an effective safety management system, as it allows for risks to be mitigated before they result in an accident or serious incident.
